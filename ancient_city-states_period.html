Ancient City-States Period

Government and Wealth Control

The government during the Pyu, Mon, and Rakhine civilizations was led by kings who controlled most wealth and resources. They stored valuable items such as gold and gemstones in royal palaces and religious sites. Temples also played an important role in managing wealth because religion and politics were closely connected.

Business and Trade Systems

Business activities were mainly based on agriculture and trade. There were no banks, so traders used barter or valuable metals to exchange goods. Trade routes connected Myanmar with India and China, bringing silk, jewelry, and pottery into the region, while exporting rice and local crafts.

Education and Daily Life

Knowledge was mostly passed down through monks and local leaders. People learned skills like farming, crafting, and trading from their families. Most citizens lived simple lives focused on farming and community work, without the need for a banking system like today.
