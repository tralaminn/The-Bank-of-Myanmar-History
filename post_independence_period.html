Post-Independence Period (1948–Present)

Government and National Banking System

After independence, the government started building a national banking system to support local economic growth. Later, during the socialist era, the government took control of all banks to manage money centrally. Today, government policies encourage stronger financial systems, private banks, and investment to support development.

Business Development and Modern Services

Business became more diverse after private banks returned in the 1990s. Companies could borrow money to grow, and international trade slowly improved. Modern banking, including digital payments, helps businesses operate faster and more efficiently.

Transition and renewed coup (2011-present)

Education about finance has improved, with schools and universities teaching economics and business subjects. In daily life, banking has become a normal part of society—people use ATMs, mobile apps, and online services to save, transfer, and spend money safely. This makes everyday life more convenient and connects Myanmar to the global economy
